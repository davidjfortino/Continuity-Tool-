Business Impact Analysis

💡
How this step works. Each row is a function the tool pre-filled from your profile. For each one, set how critical it is, its RTO (how fast it must be back) and RPO (how much data you can afford to lose), and the impact if it's down. Click the mapping button on any row to assign the people and systems behind it — that's what powers the gap analysis. Add or remove rows freely, then click Generate at the bottom.
What each column means. Criticality — how damaging an outage is (Critical → Low). RTO (Recovery Time Objective) — how quickly the function must be back. RPO (Recovery Point Objective) — how much data or work you can afford to lose (the gap back to your last good backup). Impact if down — what actually goes wrong when it stops. Mapping (👤 🗄 🤝) — click it to record the people, systems, and vendors behind the function; that's what powers the resilience analysis.

Resilience analysis

💡
How this step works. The tool reads your impact analysis and flags the weaknesses a plan on paper hides: single points of failure, people or systems stretched across too many critical functions, and recovery targets that nothing actually backs up. To fix a finding, go back to the BIA and map an owner, backup, or system. At the bottom, rate each threat in the risk register (likelihood × impact) to complete your risk assessment. Then generate the plan.

Business Continuity Plan

💡
How this step works. This is your full plan, generated from everything above. Click any section heading to expand or collapse it. Fill in the document-control, approvals, and sign-off fields at the top, and edit any recovery procedure inline. When it's ready, hit ⤓ Export plan (PDF) below — you don't have to run an exercise first. Or run a tabletop to pressure-test it before you finalize.

Tabletop exercise

Pick a scenario. The tool drops timed injects against your plan. Record decisions and flag gaps — they feed straight into the After Action Report.

💡
How this step works (optional). A tabletop is a talk-through drill. Pick a scenario and the tool throws timed events at your actual plan. In each box, type what your team would decide — and be honest about anything that would break, stall, or depend on one unavailable person. Those notes become corrective actions in the After Action Report. This step is optional; you can export the plan without it.

After Action Report

💡
How this step works. The tool drafted this report from your exercise. Set the capability rating, edit what worked and what needs improvement, and assign each corrective action an owner and a due date. When you're done, ⤓ Export full package prints everything — profile, BIA, analysis, plan, exercise, and this report — as one PDF.
